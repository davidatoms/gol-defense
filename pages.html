01 — Foundation

Conway's Game of Life

The simulation follows four rules applied simultaneously to every cell each generation. A cell is either alive (1) or dead (0). What emerges from these simple rules is the foundation everything else is built on.

▶ gif — simulation running

The base simulation — cells living, dying, and being born each generation.

03 — Structure

V / X / Y / Z Dividers

Two lines cross the center of the grid — one vertical, one horizontal — splitting the canvas into four quadrants. Each half-segment is labeled independently: V (top), X (bottom), Y (left), Z (right).

┼ gif — clicking dividers to cycle states

Clicking a label chip cycles that segment through its three states.

StateColorEffect on cells
OpenRedNo enforcement — cells evolve freely across the line
WallOrangeCells on the line are locked alive (1)
NullBlue-grey dashedCells on the line are locked dead (0)
The wall state persists through every generation step, clear, and randomize — the divider re-enforces itself after each operation.

04 — Mathematics

Mathematical Sequence Markers

Each sequence is computed from the center of the grid outward. Markers appear on the divider lines at the corresponding cell distance. Lines and boxes extend from those positions across the full grid.

π gif — sequence layers toggled on/off

Each sequence draws its own color of markers and grid lines.

Primes Twin Primes Fibonacci Perfect Squares Every 10
Primes are computed via a Sieve of Eratosthenes up to the larger of cols or rows each draw call. All sequences radiate symmetrically outward from the grid center.

07 — Interaction

Draw Box Tool

The Box button activates draw mode. Click and drag on the canvas to place a persistent rectangular boundary. Each box is labeled automatically with its date, center-relative coordinates, and a sequential letter.

⬛ gif — drawing a box

Drawing a box — label appears immediately with coordinates and date.

Labels follow Conway's naming convention: MM-DD_(col,row)_A where col/row are the top-left corner offset from the grid center.

Multiple boxes can be drawn and coexist on the grid simultaneously. Each is stored independently and persists through game steps.

08 — Boundary Control

Per-Side Hardness

Each side of a drawn box has its own independent hardness state — the same three states as the V/X/Y/Z dividers. Click a side chip on the canvas or a button in the panel below the toolbar to cycle it.
